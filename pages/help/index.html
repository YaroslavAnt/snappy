New Snap

Create: ⌘ ⇧ 2

Close: ⌘ W or double-click on the snap

Change the shortcut in Preferences->General.

Work with a Snap

Move: Drag & Drop or use arrows

Move pixel by pixel: Hold ⌥ and use arrows.

Drag & Drop: Alt + Ctrl then drag

Hide/Show snaps: ⌘ ⇧ H. You can customize this shortcut in the preferences screen.

Resize: ⌘ + or ⌘ - or drag snap corners. Reset to the original size with ⌘0.

Opacity: 0 to 9 or use contextual menu

Reset size and position: ⌘ 0

Annotations

Brush: B (5 colors, 5 sizes)

Text: T (5 colors)

Cursor: V

Eraser: E

Quick Draw: ⌘ and drag

Undo: ⌘ Z

Access the Annotations in Snappy's contextual menu (right-click on a snap).

Sharing and Storage

SnappyLink:

Store the image on our servers and share it via a unqiue URL with anyone. Storage space is unlimited but we only keep the image for 30 days.

Secure SnappyLink: Temporary snaps

Temporary snaps enable you to set a time in which the snap will self-destruct. This will permanently delete it from our servers and will make it unavailable to anyone.

Password Protected snaps:

Password protected snaps use end-to-end encryption. This means that the snap is encrypted withing Snappy, locally on your computer, and can only be decrypted in the browser by someone who has the password.

Note: We don't store passwords or the decrypted image.
Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, Evernote, Dropbox:

Simply connect your accounts and you're ready to go. We don't store any data from your account. Everything is handled on your computer.

Imgur:

Imgur doesn't require you to connect any acount. Sharing here will return an URL of the image on Imgur.

iCloud

When enabled from the preferences panel, iCloud syncronizes all of your snaps across all of your Apple devices.

Email:

Sharing over email is possible both as Snappy link and as image attachment.

iCloud

iCloud allows you to syncronize snaps across all of your Apple devices. Enabled or disable iCloud from the preferences panel.

Note: iCloud syncronization may take a while to finish

PixelPerfect mode

Toggle: ⌘ ⌥ P

PixelPerfect Mode removes the shadow from a snap. This allows you to compare visuals at pixel precision.

iPhone/iPad App

Syncronize with iCloud

Enable iCloud from the Preferences panel of the Mac App and syncing will be done automatically for you.

Create snaps

You have 3 options to create snaps on the iOS App: Camera, Library and Snapshot.

1. Tap the "+" button to take a picture or select one from the phone's library.
2. Create a snapshot of your screen with the iPhone/iPad ON-OFF Button + Home Button, pressed at the same time.
The next time you open Snappy, it will detect the screenshots and ask you if you want them imported (this options can be set to Auto from the App's settings screen).

Delete snaps

To delete simply tap the trash button in the snap's screen. Deleting a snap on the iOS App will automatically delete it from your Mac App too.

Snaps Library

Browse

This is your snap history with all snaps organized by Application name. You can search by snap title, you can filter by date and you can browse through your snaps.

Delete

To delete a snap, right click on it and select "Delete Snap". You can select multiple by holding the ⇧ key.

Open

While Snappy is in focus you can open the Snaps Library with ⌘ 1

Snap a Webpage

Snap any webpage by using "Snap Webpage Preview" in the top menu. This will open the webpage like any regular snap.

Security & Privacy

We store snaps on our server when shared with Snappy for 30 days only.

We don't store any information from any of your sharing accounts.

Snappy collects anonymous usage data and anonymous crash data.
